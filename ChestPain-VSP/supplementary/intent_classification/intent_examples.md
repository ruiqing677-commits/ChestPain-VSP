# Intent Examples

The following examples are representative utterances used to illustrate the intent categories. They are de-identified and do not contain patient-level clinical records or personal identifiers.

## Pain features

- "When did the chest pain start?"
- "Where exactly is the pain located?"
- "Does the pain radiate to your left arm or jaw?"

## Red flags

- "Do you feel short of breath?"
- "Did you faint or nearly pass out?"
- "Are you sweating heavily with the chest pain?"

## Risk/history

- "Do you have hypertension, diabetes, or high cholesterol?"
- "Have you had heart disease before?"
- "Do you smoke or have a family history of heart disease?"

## Benign / alternative clues

- "Does the pain get worse when you press on the chest wall?"
- "Do you have acid reflux or burning after meals?"
- "Do you feel anxious or panicked when the pain occurs?"
- "Does the pain change with body position?"

## Evidence requests

- "Order an ECG."
- "Check troponin."
- "Can I see the chest X-ray?"
- "Please request D-dimer and oxygen saturation."

## Reasoning / disposition

- "I think acute coronary syndrome should be considered."
- "The patient should be admitted for further evaluation."
- "This looks low risk and may be discharged with follow-up."

## Other / unclear

- "Hello."
- "Can you repeat that?"
- "Tell me more."
